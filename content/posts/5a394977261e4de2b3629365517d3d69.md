## Introduction

China’s A-share market features a well-structured market capitalization hierarchy, offering investors diverse exposure from large-cap blue chips to high-growth small and micro-cap stocks. Unlike mature overseas markets, China’s small-cap segment has a complete, standardized index system covering mid-small cap, pure small-cap, and micro-cap stocks. These small-cap indices track high-growth, niche, and specialized enterprises, known as “hidden champions” in emerging sectors, delivering higher elasticity and long-term growth potential compared with large-cap benchmarks.

This guide systematically sorts out the mainstream China small-cap indices released by CSI Index Company, National Securities Index Company, and stock exchanges, as well as their corresponding mainstream ETFs and feeder funds, providing a clear reference for global investors to allocate Chinese small-cap assets.

## Core China Small-Cap Index System

China’s mainstream small-cap indices are mainly divided into four categories: CSI series, CNI (National Securities) series, exchange-specific indices, and themed micro-cap indices. They form an intact market cap gradient from mid-small cap to ultra micro-cap, with clear segmentation and distinct stylistic characteristics.

### 1. CSI Official Small-Cap Indices (Most Authoritative & Widely Adopted)

The CSI index series is the core benchmark for A-share small-cap investment, covering the full range of mid-small, small, and micro-cap stocks with comprehensive industry diversification.

- **CSI 500 Index (000905)**: The representative mid-small cap index, consisting of the 500 largest A-shares ranked 301–800 by market capitalization after CSI 300 large-cap stocks. It features balanced industry distribution, stable fundamentals, lower volatility than pure small-cap indices, and serves as the mainstream benchmark for mid-small cap allocation.

- **CSI 1000 Index (000852)**: A pure small-cap index tracking 1,000 listed stocks ranked 801–1800 in market cap. It includes a high proportion of specialized, sophisticated, unique, and innovative enterprises, with strong growth elasticity and typical small-cap style characteristics.

- **CSI 2000 Index (000856)**: A mainstream micro-cap index covering 2,000 stocks ranked 1801–3800 by market cap. It focuses on ultra-small listed enterprises, gathers numerous niche market leaders, and boasts the highest growth potential and market sensitivity among the CSI mainstream broad-market indices.

### 2. CNI (National Securities) Small-Cap Indices

The CNI series indices focus on Shenzhen and national small-cap stocks, complementing the CSI system with unique market coverage.

- **CNI 2000 Index (399303)**: The core CNI small-cap benchmark, tracking 2,000 mid-to-small cap stocks after the top 1,000 A-shares. Its average market cap is slightly higher than the CSI 2000 Index, with relatively better liquidity and balanced risk-return characteristics.

- **SZSE Small-Cap Index (399005)**: A traditional small-cap index exclusively covering Shenzhen-listed stocks, capturing the overall performance of small-cap enterprises in the Shenzhen market.

- **ChiNext Small-Cap Index (399404)**: Focuses on small-cap stocks listed on ChiNext, highlighting the growth attributes of innovative small and medium-sized enterprises in the technology and emerging industries.

### 3. Exchange-Specific Small-Cap Indices

Launched by Shanghai and Shenzhen stock exchanges, these indices target exchange-specific small-cap segments:

- **SSE 580 Index (000158)**: The flagship small-cap benchmark of the Shanghai Stock Exchange, selecting 200 high-quality small-cap stocks after the SSE 380 mid-cap index, representing the growth of Shanghai-listed small-cap enterprises.

- **SSE 380 Index (000009)**: A classic Shanghai mid-small cap index, covering high-growth mid-cap stocks in the Shanghai market and widely regarded as a core allocation tool for Shanghai’s mid-small cap assets.

### 4. Thematic & Specialized Small-Cap Indices

These indices cover niche small-cap segments such as sci-tech innovation and micro-cap markets, catering to differentiated investment needs:

- **STAR Market 100 Index**: Tracks 100 small and medium-sized sci-tech enterprises listed on the Shanghai STAR Market, focusing on high-end manufacturing, biomedicine, and semiconductor small-cap stocks.

- **NEEQ 50 Index**: The core benchmark for the Beijing Stock Exchange, covering the 50 largest and most liquid listed enterprises on NEEQ, all of which belong to the ultra-small cap segment with high growth attributes.

- **Wind Micro-Cap Index**: Tracks the smallest batch of tradable A-shares, representing the extreme micro-cap style with the highest market volatility and growth flexibility.

## Key Investment Insights for China Small-Cap Indices

### 1. Clear Market Cap & Risk Gradient

The risk and return potential increase sequentially along the market cap gradient:**CSI 500 (stable mid-small cap) → CSI 1000 (growth small cap) → CNI 2000 → CSI 2000 (micro cap) → Wind Micro-Cap (ultra micro cap)**. Investors can select targets according to their risk tolerance.

### 2. Liquidity & Cost Principles

Publicly traded ETFs feature higher liquidity and lower management fees, suitable for short-term trading and long-term position holding; OTC feeder funds support automatic fixed investment, ideal for retail investors’ long-term asset accumulation. Large-scale flagship ETFs effectively reduce tracking error and liquidity risk.

### 3. Style & Market Coverage Differences

CSI series indices cover Shanghai and Shenzhen A-shares with full market coverage; CNI and Shenzhen series indices tilt toward Shenzhen-listed stocks; SSE series focus on Shanghai small-cap assets. Sci-tech themed small-cap indices such as STAR Market 100 are concentrated in high-tech tracks with strong policy dividend attributes.

### 4. Long-Term Value Logic

China’s small and micro-cap listed enterprises are mostly niche industry leaders and specialized innovative enterprises. Benefiting from national policies supporting small and medium-sized tech enterprises, they have ample room for performance growth and valuation restoration in the long run, making them an important supplement to large-cap blue chip asset allocation.

## Conclusion

China has formed a mature, multi-dimensional small-cap index and fund system, covering all segments from mid-small cap to ultra micro-cap. For global investors, small-cap indices represented by CSI 500, CSI 1000, and CSI 2000 provide diversified tools to capture the growth dividends of China’s grassroots innovative enterprises. By matching appropriate indices and fund products based on risk preferences and investment cycles, investors can efficiently layout the high-growth track of China’s A-share small-cap market.
